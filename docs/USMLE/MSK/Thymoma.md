A tumor of the thymus gland. An asymptomatic thymoma is usually detected as an incidental finding on a chest x-ray, where it appears as a mediastinal mass. Symptomatic thymomas typically present in individuals between 40â€“60 years of age with thoracic symptoms (e.g., chest pain, cough, dyspnea) or paraneoplastic syndromes, the most common of which is [[./Myasthenia gravis|Myasthenia gravis]]. Treatment options include surgery, chemotherapy, and/or radiotherapy.
